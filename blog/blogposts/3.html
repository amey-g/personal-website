BITS requires students to take up Humanities Electives in their sophomore year to be able to graduate. Which in my honest opinion is a great thing as The Humanities often get neglected in a Engineering Student's life but the are some of the most important things to learn for an overall development of a student. I took up to HuEls this semester namely CALC (Critical Analysis of Literature and Cinema) and Cinematic Adaptation. I remember starting to watch classic cult films back in 9th grade and I have been a cinephile ever since. Honestly, these two are the courses which I am currently enjoying the most to sit through. In Cinematic Adaptation we are required to choose any movie based upon some form of literature and present an analysis of that movie, I'm really into this kind of stuff and am thoroughly enjoying it. Let's see what this semester has for me.
